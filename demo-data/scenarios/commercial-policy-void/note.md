## Clinical history

Patient B is a 55-year-old individual with a 6-year history of psoriatic arthritis
(PsA). The diagnosis was established by a board-certified rheumatologist following
confirmation of psoriasis plaques involving greater than 15% body surface area (BSA)
and inflammatory arthritis of the hands and feet meeting CASPAR criteria. No
peripheral joint imaging beyond plain radiographs has been performed; radiographs of
the hands and feet obtained at diagnosis showed soft-tissue swelling without erosive
changes, consistent with early disease. MRI of the sacroiliac joints and lumbar spine
has not been ordered.

Disease course: the patient has completed a 12-week trial of naproxen 500 mg BID
(discontinued for GI intolerance, documented in chart) and a 24-week trial of
methotrexate (MTX) 20 mg/week, which was discontinued after inadequate response
defined as continued tender joint count ≥6 and active plaque involvement despite
treatment adherence confirmed by pharmacy fill history. Leflunomide was not initiated
due to patient-reported plans for future pregnancy (counseled and documented).

Current disease burden: 8 tender joints, 6 swollen joints; DAPSA score 38 (high
disease activity); PASI score 14.2 (moderate plaque burden). No uveitis. No dactylitis
at present. Axial symptoms are absent by history.

## Clinical justification

The treating rheumatologist has determined that Patient B meets criteria for biologic
DMARD therapy for psoriatic arthritis. Two conventional therapy courses have been
trialed:

1. Naproxen 500 mg BID (12 weeks) — discontinued due to GI intolerance (nausea and
   epigastric pain), documented in office notes.
2. Methotrexate 20 mg/week (24 weeks) — discontinued due to inadequate response;
   pharmacy fill history confirms adherence.

Etanercept is an FDA-approved biologic for active psoriatic arthritis and carries an
FDA-labeled indication for this condition in adult patients who have had an inadequate
response to conventional DMARD therapy. The Aetna commercial drug list (Open Access
Elect Choice POS, 2026-04 revision) includes etanercept as a covered specialty
biologic.

The clinical evidence base — including the FDA-approved prescribing information — fully
supports this prior-authorization request. The patient has no contraindications to
etanercept identified in the FDA label.
