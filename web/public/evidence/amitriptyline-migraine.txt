Amitriptyline for Migraine Prophylaxis — Clinical Evidence Summary

Indication under review: amitriptyline for the prevention (prophylaxis) of migraine in adults.

EVIDENCE OF BENEFIT
A systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (Jackson et al.,
BMJ 2010; PMID 20961988) found that tricyclic antidepressants, including amitriptyline,
significantly reduced the frequency and severity of migraine headaches compared with
placebo, with the benefit increasing the longer treatment continued.

GUIDELINE SUPPORT
The American Academy of Neurology and American Headache Society evidence-based guideline
on pharmacologic migraine prevention identifies amitriptyline as an effective option
recommended for migraine prophylaxis.

CONCLUSION
On this evidence, amitriptyline is a recognized, compendia-supported pharmacologic option
for migraine prophylaxis in adults when first-line measures are inadequate.
