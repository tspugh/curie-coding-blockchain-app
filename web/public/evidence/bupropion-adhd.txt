Bupropion for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) — Clinical Evidence Summary

Indication under review: bupropion for the treatment of ADHD in adults.

EVIDENCE OF BENEFIT
A Cochrane systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials
(Verbeeck W, Bekkering GE, Van den Noortgate W, Kramers C. Cochrane Database of
Systematic Reviews 2017; PMID 28965364) found that bupropion decreased the severity
of ADHD symptoms and increased the proportion of patients achieving a significant
clinical response compared with placebo, indicating a benefit of bupropion for the
treatment of adult ADHD.

COMPENDIA AND GUIDELINE SUPPORT
Bupropion is supported by recognized clinical compendia and treatment guidelines as a
second-line or alternative pharmacologic option for ADHD, particularly when stimulant
therapy is contraindicated, not tolerated, or carries a risk of misuse.

CONCLUSION
Bupropion is supported by recognized clinical compendia and guidelines for the
treatment of ADHD, with systematic-review evidence demonstrating a reduction in ADHD
symptom severity. Coverage of bupropion for this indication is clinically supported.
