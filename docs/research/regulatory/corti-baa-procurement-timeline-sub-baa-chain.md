## 2026-05-17 — Corti BAA: no self-serve path confirmed; 2–6 week enterprise timeline; Hospital→cliqueue→Corti sub-BAA chain legally valid under 45 CFR 164.502(e)(1)(ii); cliqueue should act as primary BA

**Question investigated:** What is Corti's standard BAA turnaround time for enterprise procurement, and can cliqueue pre-negotiate a standard form BAA for hospital onboarding rather than requiring each hospital to negotiate directly with Corti?

- **No Corti self-serve or click-through BAA exists.** Corti's public-facing pages (safety, trust center, help.corti.app, corti.ai/legal) do not describe a click-through or downloadable standard form BAA. Enterprise procurement requires contacting Corti's sales/legal team directly. This is consistent with findings from the 2026-05-14 Corti PHI data-flow investigation: "No publicly downloadable BAA template exists on Corti's website. A BAA must be negotiated as part of enterprise procurement, reachable via `privacy@corti.ai` or the enterprise sales team." ([Corti Safety](https://www.corti.ai/safety); [Corti Terms & Conditions](https://assistant.corti.ai/terms-and-conditions))

- **Typical enterprise BAA turnaround for AI healthcare SaaS vendors: 2–6 weeks** on the standard enterprise path. Fast path (vendor accepts hospital's standard paper with minimal edits): 2–7 business days. Slow path (large health system procurement queue, new vendor, contested indemnity or training-data clauses): 1–3 months. No vendor — including Corti, Nuance DAX Copilot, AWS Comprehend Medical — publicly discloses a BAA turnaround SLA. ([HIPAAJournal: HIPAA BAA guide](https://www.hipaajournal.com/hipaa-business-associate-agreement/); [Accountable HQ: BAA requirements guide](https://www.accountablehq.com/post/hipaa-business-associate-agreement-requirements-complete-guide-for-covered-entities); industry synthesis — no single primary citation)

- **Major cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) offer self-serve BAAs.** AWS provides a standard BAA via AWS Artifact / account workflow, often activatable in days for existing enterprise customers. Azure provides a standard HIPAA BAA via the Microsoft Trust Center, often self-serve for cloud customers. Corti's own Azure-hosted infrastructure means Corti itself has a cloud-vendor BAA with Azure — but this does not extend downstream to Corti's product customers. ([AWS HIPAA compliance](https://aws.amazon.com/compliance/hipaa-compliance/); [Microsoft Trust Center HIPAA](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/trust-center/privacy/hipaa))

- **The Hospital→cliqueue→Corti sub-BAA chain is legally valid under HIPAA.** 45 CFR 164.502(e)(1)(ii) permits a covered entity to disclose PHI to a business associate without a direct BAA with the BA's subcontractors, provided the BA (cliqueue) obtains written assurances from the subcontractor (Corti) requiring the same HIPAA restrictions. 45 CFR 164.308(b)(2) extends this to all downstream subcontractors. HHS FAQ confirms that a BA must require subcontractors to agree to the same restrictions as the BA — but the covered entity does not need to be party to the subcontractor agreement. ([45 CFR 164.502(e)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-C/part-164/section-164.502); [45 CFR 164.308(b)](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-45/subtitle-A/subchapter-C/part-164/section-164.308); [HHS: Business Associates — Subcontractors](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/privacy/guidance/business-associates/subcontractors/index.html); [Paubox: When AI becomes a BA](https://www.paubox.com/blog/when-does-ai-become-a-business-associate-under-hipaa))

- **Cliqueue signing the master cliqueue↔Corti BAA is the correct onboarding architecture.** If cliqueue holds a single executed BAA with Corti (cliqueue as BA, Corti as BA subcontractor), then each hospital onboarding only needs to execute a BAA with cliqueue — not with Corti directly. This compresses the hospital-facing BAA surface from two negotiations (hospital↔cliqueue + hospital↔Corti) to one (hospital↔cliqueue). The hospital BAA with cliqueue must explicitly: (a) require cliqueue to maintain sub-BAAs with all PHI-touching subcontractors, (b) disclose Corti as a named subprocessor, (c) flow down HIPAA Security Rule requirements, and (d) require breach notification within the contractual window. ([HHS FAQ 304: BA subcontractors](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/faq/304/are-business-associates-required-to-obtain-satisfactory-assurances-from-their-business-associate-subcontractors/index.html))

- **AI-specific BAA clauses are mandatory and commonly under-negotiated.** AI vendor BAAs commonly lack: (a) explicit prohibition on training general models on hospital PHI, (b) subprocessor list disclosure with audit rights, (c) 24–72 hour breach notification (vs. HIPAA's 60-day floor), (d) data deletion/return terms at contract end, (e) liability cap adequacy. The 2026 HIPAA Security Rule NPRM (if finalized, expected Summer 2026) adds mandatory annual BA verification requirements — meaning cliqueue's BAA with Corti must include an annual audit/attestation right. ([Scribing.io: BAA requirements for AI medical scribes](https://www.scribing.io/blog/baa-requirements-ai-medical-scribes); [HIPAA Journal: 2026 HIPAA Security Rule NPRM](https://www.hipaajournal.com/hipaa-updates-hipaa-changes/))

- **Operational risk: Corti BAA procurement is a pre-launch blocker.** Because cliqueue's hospital agent transmits clinical notes to Corti Symphony before any on-chain operation, the cliqueue↔Corti BAA must be executed before any hospital pilot begins. An unexecuted BAA makes every API call a HIPAA violation. Given the 2–6 week enterprise BAA timeline, cliqueue must initiate BAA negotiation with Corti (`privacy@corti.ai`) as a pre-pilot task, not an onboarding task. A reasonable pre-launch schedule: BAA initiated ≥6 weeks before first hospital pilot; standard form offered by Corti reviewed by cliqueue counsel within 2 weeks; redlines resolved within 4 weeks; execution before code deployment.

**Design implication:** cliqueue should negotiate and execute its own master BAA with Corti (cliqueue as BA, Corti as BA subcontractor) before any hospital pilot — making Corti a named subprocessor in cliqueue's standard hospital BAA rather than requiring hospitals to independently contract with Corti. This single architectural decision compresses hospital onboarding from two BAA negotiations to one, removing a potential 2–6 week per-hospital blocking dependency. The hospital BAA must include a "Subprocessor Exhibit" listing Corti explicitly, with flow-down requirements and annual verification rights.
