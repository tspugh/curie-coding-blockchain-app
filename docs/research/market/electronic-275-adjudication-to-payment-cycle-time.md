# Electronic 275 Attachment: Adjudication-to-Payment Cycle Time Research

## 2026-05-17 — No payer companion guide publishes adjudication-to-payment cycle time for electronic 275 vs. paper/fax; regulatory framework explains the absence; correct ROI framing is clock-pause reduction not adjudication speed

**Question investigated:** Does any payer companion guide (UHC, Aetna, Cigna, Humana, Anthem) publish the adjudication-to-payment cycle time specifically for claims with electronic 275 attachments vs. paper/fax — enabling cliqueue to cite a full end-to-end comparison beyond the 5–7 day administrative matching window alone?

**Key findings:**

- **No payer companion guide publishes a days-to-payment benchmark for electronic 275 claims vs. paper/fax.** Extensive review of UHC, Anthem, Cigna, Aetna, Humana companion guides and major clearinghouse (Waystar, Availity, Optum) materials found no published adjudication-to-payment cycle time that isolates the 275 attachment variable. This is a confirmed absence, not a search gap. ([Waystar claim attachments](https://www.waystar.com/our-platform/claim-management/claim-attachments/); [Waystar three benefits blog](https://www.waystar.com/blog-three-benefits-of-switching-to-electronic-claim-attachments/))

- **The regulatory framework explains why no benchmark exists: the adjudication clock is paused during documentation development.** Under Medicare FFS, claims requiring documentation are "other-than-clean" — the 45-day adjudication deadline is paused on the day the contractor sends a development letter and resumes only when documentation is received. Under state prompt pay laws (Arizona, Texas, others), commercial payers similarly pause the adjudication clock during documentation requests. Electronic 275 reduces the duration of the pause (attachment delivered in minutes vs. weeks/months of fax/mail), not the adjudication speed after receipt. ([HHS timeliness standards for other-than-clean claims](https://www.hhs.gov/guidance/document/timeliness-standards-processing-other-clean-claims-1); [Texas Prompt Pay FAQ](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/hprovider/ppsb418faq.html); [Arizona 20-3102](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/20/03102.htm))

- **Medicare FFS clean claim vs. other-than-clean distinction is load-bearing for ROI framing.** Clean claims: paid within 30 days (payment floor 14 days for electronic). Other-than-clean claims (requiring documentation): 45-day deadline paused during development, clock resumes on receipt. Once documentation is received via electronic 275, the remaining 45-day adjudication period applies identically to paper receipt — no payer or regulator publishes a shorter post-receipt adjudication period for electronically-received attachments vs. mail. ([CMS prompt payment guidelines](https://legalclarity.org/cms-prompt-payment-guidelines-deadlines-and-interest/); [CMS MLN MM3557](https://www.cms.gov/Outreach-and-Education/Medicare-Learning-Network-MLN/MLNMattersArticles/downloads/MM3557.pdf))

- **CMS-0053-F's $781.98M projected annual savings is labor-cost savings, not cycle time reduction.** The regulatory impact analysis quantifies savings as eliminating the $22/transaction manual processing cost (8 minutes per transaction per CAQH CORE estimate) across 181 million annual attachments — not any reduction in days-to-payment. ([CMS-0053-F fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/administrative-simplification-adoption-standards-health-care-claims-attachments-transactions); [Thessigroup CAQH CORE data](https://thessigroup.com/blog/advancing-275-electronic-attachments-in-medical-billing-a-move-towards-efficiency/))

- **Waystar "AR days" claim is unquantified vendor marketing.** Waystar states electronic attachments "strengthen cash flow and significantly reduce AR days" but publishes no specific day-count improvement in public-facing materials. This claim is marketing language, not a citable primary source. ([Waystar claim attachments page](https://www.waystar.com/our-platform/claim-management/claim-attachments/))

- **The correct ROI chain for cliqueue is now fully specified from primary sources:** (1) 60–120 day paper/fax documentation-pending window (Kodiak Solutions VP Matt Szaflarski, FAH podcast — 89% of RFI denials resolve without net revenue leakage, but not until 60/90/120 days); (2) 5–7 day payer matching/intake window for electronic 275 (UHC and Anthem companion guides); (3) adjudication period after documentation received: 14–45 days (Medicare FFS, payer-contract dependent for commercial) — this component is identical for electronic vs. paper once received. The 10–20× documented compression is the documentation-pending window (step 1→2), not the adjudication speed (step 3).

- **No vendor, regulator, or industry body (HFMA, CAQH CORE, WEDI) publishes a full end-to-end days-to-payment comparison that isolates electronic 275 vs. paper/fax submission.** The absence is confirmed across CAQH 2023 and 2024 Index Reports, HFMA denial metrics guidance, CMS-0053-F regulatory impact analysis, and vendor materials.

**Design implication:** cliqueue's ROI calculator should present the attachment value as two explicitly separated components: (A) "Documentation-pending window: 60–120 days paper/fax → 5–7 day payer matching window (Kodiak/FAH + UHC/Anthem companion guides)" — this is the primary, fully sourced ROI claim; (B) "Adjudication period after documentation received: 14–45 days (payer-contract dependent, identical for electronic vs. paper) — not reduced by 275 adoption." The ROI story remains strong for component A (10–20× compression) but component B must not be overstated as also being accelerated.

**Open questions generated:**
1. Should cliqueue's hospital onboarding ROI calculator explicitly label the two components ("documentation-pending window" and "post-receipt adjudication period") — so hospital finance teams understand that electronic 275 eliminates the 60–120 day float, not the adjudication period itself?
2. Is there a private payer that publishes a specific adjudication SLA for claims submitted with a proactive unsolicited 275 (vs. claims where the payer must issue a 277 RFI) — enabling cliqueue to cite a post-receipt adjudication advantage for unsolicited submission?
3. At what AR days threshold (e.g., claims >90 days outstanding due to documentation pending) does cliqueue's value proposition create a measurable Days-Sales-Outstanding improvement that hospital CFOs can calculate from their own benchmarks — enabling a hospital-specific ROI calculation without relying on published benchmarks?
