## 2026-05-14 — Annual US spend on ICD-10 coding and documented denial losses

### Market size — medical coding (including ICD-10)

- The US medical coding market was valued at **$21.9–$27.5 billion in 2024**, growing at ~10% CAGR through 2033 (estimates vary by analyst firm).
  — [Grand View Research: US Medical Coding Market](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-medical-coding-market); [SkyQuestT](https://www.skyquestt.com/report/u-s-medical-coding-market)
- ICD-coded claims represent the largest segment: **>69% of US medical coding revenue** in 2024, driven by mandatory CMS reporting.
  — [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-medical-coding-market)
- Hospital revenue cycle activities (of which coding is a subset) cost **>$160 billion per year** across the industry; administrative costs exceed 40% of total hospital spending.
  — [McKinsey: Revenue Cycle Management to Revenue Excellence](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/healthcare/our-insights/from-revenue-cycle-management-to-revenue-excellence)

### Computer-Assisted Coding (CAC) adoption and costs

- More than **68% of US hospitals with 400+ beds** have implemented CAC systems.
  — [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-medical-coding-market)
- NLP-based CAC platforms are integrated into **72% of large hospital networks**, reducing manual coding time by ~25%.
  — [Market.us: US Medical Coding Market 2025](https://media.market.us/u-s-medical-coding-market-news-2025/)
- CAC can increase coder productivity by **11–30%** (3M/Solventum survey).
  — [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/us-medical-coding-market)
- CAC licensing and support for a small health system (~1,000 beds) can exceed **$500,000/year**.
  — [emrguides: Medical Billing Service Cost](https://emrguides.com/medical-billing-service-cost/)

### Per-claim coding cost breakdown

- Outsourced full-service medical coding: **$4–$10 per claim** (most services cluster at $4–$7), fixed fee per encounter.
  — [MBC: How Much Does Medical Billing Cost?](https://www.medicalbillersandcoders.com/blog/how-much-does-medical-billing-cost/)
- Processing a **clean claim** costs ~**$6.50**; a **denied claim** costs **$25–$181 to rework** (median ~$25–$30).
  — [Aptarro: US Healthcare Denial Rates & Reimbursement Statistics](https://www.aptarro.com/insights/us-healthcare-denial-rates-reimbursement-statistics)
- In-house billing/coding staff: **$90,000–$140,000 per biller per year** (salary + benefits + software + training + overhead).
  — [BestMedicalBilling: Medical Billing Services Cost](https://bestmedicalbilling.com/blogs/medical-billing-services-cost-pricing/)
- Coder salary benchmarks (2024): median ~$50,250 (BLS); certified coders ~$66,979; risk-adjustment specialists up to $99,214.
  — [AHIMA productivity study via MedLearn/ICD10Monitor](https://icd10monitor.medlearn.com/ahima-coding-productivity-study-and-preparing-for-icd-11/)
- Historical AHIMA inpatient coding productivity: ~**3 records/hour** (20 min/record) — still frequently cited as the operational baseline.
  — [AHIMA Coding Productivity Study](https://icd10monitor.medlearn.com/ahima-coding-productivity-study-and-preparing-for-icd-11/)

### Claim denial rates and financial losses

- Initial claim denial rate reached **11.81% in 2024**, up from ~9% in 2016 and 10.2% in prior years.
  — [Experian Health: State of Claims Report 2025](https://www.experian.com/blogs/healthcare/healthcare-claim-denials-statistics-state-of-claims-report/)
- **$262 billion in claims were denied in 2024** industry-wide; **84% of those denials are estimated to be potentially avoidable**.
  — [AZebraTech: The Real Cost of Claim Denials](https://azebratech.com/blog/healthcare-claim-denial-automation/)
- Hospitals lose an average of **4.8% of net patient revenue** to unresolved denials; the figure can reach 5–10% of gross revenue.
  — [Aptarro: US Healthcare Denial Rates & Reimbursement Statistics](https://www.aptarro.com/insights/us-healthcare-denial-rates-reimbursement-statistics)
- Unresolved denials represent an average annual loss of **$5 million** for a single hospital (up to 5% of net patient revenue).
  — [AHIMA: Claims Denials — A Step-by-Step Approach](https://journal.ahima.org/page/claims-denials-a-step-by-step-approach-to-resolution)
- The average **coding-related denial dollar amount rose 126% in 2024** to $631 per denied claim (up from $297 in 2023).
  — [AAPC: Claims Denials Are on the Rise](https://www.aapc.com/blog/91574-claims-denials-are-on-the-rise/)
- Administrative cost per denied claim rose from $43.84 (2022) to **$57.23 (2023)**, not counting the lost revenue itself.
  — [Experian Health: State of Claims Report 2025](https://www.experian.com/blogs/healthcare/healthcare-claim-denials-statistics-state-of-claims-report/)
- By end of 2023, **15% of initial claims were denied** for payment (McKinsey, citing industry data).
  — [McKinsey: Three Ways to Ease Pressure on Health System Revenue Cycles](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/healthcare/our-insights/healthcare-blog/three-ways-to-ease-the-pressure-on-health-system-revenue-cycles)
- Prior authorizations and missing/inaccurate data are the leading denial reasons; coding errors are a contributing but distinct category.
  — [Experian Health: State of Claims Report 2025](https://www.experian.com/blogs/healthcare/healthcare-claim-denials-statistics-state-of-claims-report/)

### AI autonomous coding vendors — accuracy claims (as of August 2025, KLAS Research)

- **Fathom**: 95.5/100 KLAS performance score; customers validate **≥90% automation rates at ≥98.3% coding accuracy** (Your Health deployment, Oct 2025). Available in Epic Toolbox. Named #1 for Reducing Cost of Care in KLAS 2025 Emerging Solutions Top 20.

  — [PR Newswire: CodaMetrix Best in KLAS](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/codametrix-named-no-1-in-inaugural-best-in-klas-title-for-autonomous-medical-coding-302678539.html)
- **Nym Health**: **>95% accuracy** in patient chart coding; rated a Top Performer by KLAS. Strongest in emergency department and radiology.
  — [BusinessWire: Nym KLAS Top Performer](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250820930915/en/Nym-Rated-a-Top-Performer-by-KLAS-in-First-Report-Dedicated-to-Autonomous-Medical-Coding)

  — [AKASA: Revenue Cycle Trends 2024](https://akasa.com/blog/revenue-cycle-trends/)
- KLAS August 2025 report ("Autonomous Coding 2025: A Promising Start for an Early Market"): autonomous coding is **most prevalent in radiology and emergency departments**; market is in early stages but delivering measurable workflow efficiency.

**Design implication:** The $262B/year denial pool (84% potentially avoidable) and the 126% jump in coding-related denial dollar amounts are the sharpest market-pain anchors for cliqueue-coding-blockchain. The protocol's value proposition is not just faster coding but an immutable audit trail that collapses the dispute cycle — replacing the $57/denied-claim rework loop with on-chain attestation that prevents the dispute from arising. The market is large ($21–27B coding spend), growing, and actively automating, but the settlement layer remains EDI-based and paper-like.

## 2026-05-17 — Denial rework cycle economics confirmed: $25.7B adjudication cost (2023), $57.23/denied claim, 45–180 days rework window, and the timely-filing window risk

### Total hospital claims adjudication cost and per-claim rework economics

- **Hospital claims adjudication costs reached $25.7 billion in 2023**, a 23% YoY increase from $19.7B in 2022. Of that, **~$18 billion is potentially unnecessary expense** attributable to denials that were ultimately overturned on appeal.
  — [Premier Inc. Claims Adjudication Study, 2024](https://premierinc.com/newsroom/blog/claims-adjudication-costs-providers-25-7-billion)
- **Per-denied-claim administrative rework cost rose from $43.84 (2022) to $57.23 (2023)** (labor accounts for 90% of processing expenses). This is the processing overhead only — it does not include the value of the denied claim itself.
  — [Premier Inc. Claims Adjudication Study, 2024](https://premierinc.com/newsroom/blog/claims-adjudication-costs-providers-25-7-billion)
- **Providers averaged three rounds of review** per successfully overturned denial, with each cycle taking **45–60 days**. Total resolution time for a denied-then-overturned claim: commonly **135–180 days** (3 cycles × 45–60 days).
  — [Premier Inc. Claims Adjudication Study, 2024](https://premierinc.com/newsroom/blog/claims-adjudication-costs-providers-25-7-billion)
- **Prior authorization post-approval denials** (payer denies despite PA being obtained) jumped from 3.2% to **10.4% of all denied claims** in 2023 — a 225% increase. These are categorically different from coding-error denials: they require a different rework path (clinical appeal vs. code correction).
  — [Premier Inc. Claims Adjudication Study, 2024](https://premierinc.com/newsroom/blog/claims-adjudication-costs-providers-25-7-billion)

### Time-to-payment: clean vs. denied claims

- **Clean claims** (no denial, no rework): typically paid in **15–30 days** for electronic Medicare/commercial claims. Medicare Advantage plans: **30–45 days** on average; traditional Medicare: **10–14 days**.
  — [HFMA RFI report (Kodiak Solutions, Aug 2024)](https://www.hfma.org/revenue-cycle/report-payer-requests-for-information-are-slowing-reimbursement/)
- **RFI-denied claims** (Request for Information denial, the fastest-growing denial type): payer has **30 days to decide** whether to deny, then **60 additional days** to research and pay. Total additional delay: **90 days** minimum before the claim resolves.
  — [HFMA / Kodiak Solutions "Death by a thousand requests" (Aug 2024)](https://www.hfma.org/revenue-cycle/report-payer-requests-for-information-are-slowing-reimbursement/)
- **RFI denial dollar volume** (Kodiak Solutions benchmark participants): **$6 billion delayed Jan–May 2024**, annualizing to **$14.4 billion** in 2024 — up from $11B annualized in 2022. RFI denial rate of billed charges: **3.82% in 2024** (up from 3.51% in 2022).
  — [HFMA / Kodiak Solutions "Death by a thousand requests" (Aug 2024)](https://www.hfma.org/revenue-cycle/report-payer-requests-for-information-are-slowing-reimbursement/)
- **True AR days increased 5.2% YoY in 2024**, driven by rising RFI denials and slower payer responses. Average performers now have **>36% of AR over 90 days** (vs. 15–20% benchmark).
  — [Revecore: Health System Denials and Underpayments 2026](https://revecore.com/health-system-denials-underpayments-2026/)

### Timely-filing window risk from denial rework cycles

- **Commercial payer timely-filing windows** are commonly **90–180 days from date of service**. A corrected claim or appeal generally does **not** restart the original timely-filing clock — providers must manage the rework cycle within the original window.
  — [HFMA / industry RCM literature]
- With 45–60 days per review cycle and providers averaging three rounds, a claim first denied near the end of a 90-day timely-filing window **is at material risk of expiration** if rework is not initiated within the first 30 days of denial discovery.
  — Derived from Premier per-cycle timing + commercial timely-filing window norms
- **Medicare standard timely-filing deadline is 12 months from date of service** (not 90 days). This provides significantly more runway than commercial plans, reducing Medicare-specific timely-filing expiration risk. However, the 90-day window commonly cited in RCM literature refers to commercial payer contracts, not Medicare.
  — [CMS: Medicare Timely Filing Requirements](https://www.cms.gov/)

### Coding errors as the top named denial reason (qualitative confirmation)

- Premier's 2023 study names **"minor clerical/data errors including coding mistakes, misspelled names, missing information, inverted numbers"** as the **single top reason to deny claims**. No percentage is given.
  — [Premier Inc. Claims Adjudication Study, 2024](https://premierinc.com/newsroom/blog/claims-adjudication-costs-providers-25-7-billion)
- Massachusetts Health Policy Commission (2024 state-level data): **4.9% of all claims were denied** specifically for "incomplete claim, coding error, or duplicate claim or coverage" — a combined bucket. This is consistent with coding errors being a mid-tier share of total denial volume at the state level.
  — [WBJournal / Massachusetts HPC 2024](https://wbjournal.com/article/health-policy-commission-health-insurers-denied-20-of-claims-in-2024/)
- No primary national source publishes a single percentage isolating ICD-10 coding errors from the broader coding/documentation/clerical denial bucket. The **~20% coding-error share** cited in the prior 2026-05-14 entry (MDaudit 2024) remains the best available estimate for coding errors as a share of total denial volume.

**Design implication:** The Premier data confirms cliqueue's core ROI thesis: the rework cycle (3 rounds × 45–60 days = 135–180 days per overturned denial, at $57.23 administrative overhead per claim) is the friction that on-chain settlement directly compresses. For a hospital submitting 10,000 claims/month with a 12% denial rate (~1,200 denials), the coding-error subset (~240 claims at 20% share) currently costs $13,752/month in pure overhead plus 135–180 days of float. An on-chain settlement layer that eliminates the coding-dispute rework loop (via immutable attestation and pre-negotiated adjudication) converts that to near-zero rework cost and same-day finality. The $14.4B RFI denial pipeline (2024) is a parallel opportunity: cliqueue's audit trail can pre-empt RFI denials by providing payers with cryptographic proof of clinical record linkage at claim submission time.
