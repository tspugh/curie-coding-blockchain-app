## 2026-05-17 — FHIR-based claim submission: no payer has replaced EDI 837; CMS-0057-F mandates prior-auth and data-access APIs only; Da Vinci IGs remain recommended not required

**Question investigated:** Are major US payers (UHC, Aetna, Anthem/Elevance, Humana, Cigna) actively deploying FHIR-based claim submission or adjudication APIs as of 2025–2026 — and does this create a viable non-EDI integration path for cliqueue's payer agent by 2027?

- **No major US payer has launched a production FHIR R4 `Claim`/`ClaimResponse` API for direct provider claim submission replacing EDI 837P/837I as of May 2026.** Publicly exposed payer FHIR APIs are scoped to: patient access, coverage, formulary, provider directory, prior authorization status, and explanation of benefits (EOB). None of the five largest national payers (UHC, Aetna, Anthem, Humana, Cigna) publicly document a production FHIR Claims submission endpoint. ([Perplexity synthesis, May 2026])

- **CMS-0057-F (Interoperability and Prior Authorization Final Rule) is NOT a claim-submission replacement mandate.** The rule requires five FHIR-based APIs — Patient Access, Provider Access, Provider Directory, Payer-to-Payer, and Prior Authorization — all live in production by **January 1, 2027**. These APIs cover data exchange and prior-auth workflows; the rule explicitly does not require FHIR-based claim submission or any replacement of the HIPAA X12 837 standard. ([CMS-0057-F final rule](https://www.cms.gov/priorities/burden-reduction/overview/interoperability/policies-regulations/cms-interoperability-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f); [Firely CMS-0057-F breakdown](https://fire.ly/blog/cms-0057-f-decoded-must-have-apis-vs-nice-to-have-igs-for-2026-2027/))

- **Da Vinci implementation guides (CDex, PDex, PAS) are recommended but not mandated by CMS-0057-F.** The rule specifies FHIR R4 + US Core + SMART/OAuth2 as the technical baseline; specific IGs like CARIN Blue Button, Da Vinci PDex, and Da Vinci Prior Authorization Support (PAS) are listed as "nice to have" rather than required conformance targets. ([Firely CMS-0057-F breakdown](https://fire.ly/blog/cms-0057-f-decoded-must-have-apis-vs-nice-to-have-igs-for-2026-2027/))

- **Da Vinci CDex (Clinical Data Exchange) for claim attachments has not reached confirmed production at any named payer.** HL7's January 2026 Da Vinci project update describes "substantial progress" and an active pilot program (Trebuchet) covering CDex, PDex, and Prior Auth IGs — but names no payer as having completed production deployment of CDex for claims-supporting clinical documentation exchange. The HIMSS25 pilot (94% immediate prior-auth resolution, MultiCare + Regence) is the most concrete named example, covering prior auth specifically, not claim attachments. ([HL7 Da Vinci 2025 progress](https://hl7news.hl7.org/2026/01/06/hl7-da-vinci-project-advances-transformational-work-in-2025/))

- **No finalized CMS or HIPAA Administrative Simplification rule mandates FHIR claim submission by any deadline.** The X12 837 transaction standard remains the sole federally mandated claim submission format under HIPAA. A separate electronic attachments rulemaking (which would cover FHIR-based CDex Attachments IG) has not been finalized as of May 2026. ([CMS-0057-F fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/cms-interoperability-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f); [Federal Register CMS-0057-F](https://www.federalregister.gov/public-inspection/2024-00895/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-patient-protection-and-affordable-care-act-advancing-interoperability))

- **CAQH CORE Phase IV operating rules are EDI/X12-centric, not FHIR-based.** CORE's operating rules govern administrative connectivity, acknowledgment standards, eligibility/benefit inquiry, claim status, and referral/authorization transactions — all rooted in the HIPAA EDI framework. CORE has not produced a Phase IV operating-rule framework for FHIR-based real-time claim adjudication. ([Perplexity synthesis, May 2026])

- **The practical payer integration landscape through at least 2027 remains EDI 837 for claim submission and EDI 835 for remittance.** FHIR APIs at major payers are live or becoming live for prior-auth (mandatory by Jan 2027) and member data access — not for claim submission. A cliqueue payer agent that bypasses the EDI clearinghouse layer for claim submission has no payer-side FHIR API to connect to.

**Design implication:** cliqueue's payer agent must continue to treat EDI 837 (submission) and EDI 835 (remittance) as the integration target through at least 2027–2028. The off-chain EDI adapter is not an interim workaround — it is a durable architectural requirement with no near-term FHIR replacement on the payer side. The CMS-0057-F Prior Authorization API (mandatory by Jan 2027) is the only FHIR surface cliqueue should monitor for near-term payer integration, and only for prior-auth pre-submission workflows (not claim submission or adjudication). The Trebuchet/CDex pilot space is worth tracking annually but should not influence MVP architecture.

## 2026-05-17 — CMS-0053-F finalizes HIPAA claims attachments via X12 275/277 + HL7 CDA (not FHIR CDex); Da Vinci CDex STU 2.1.0 unincorporated; EDI adapter is a durable long-term requirement through at least 2028; `PayerAdapterInterface` seam is confirmed architecture

**Question investigated:** Has CMS published a proposed rulemaking or final rule for FHIR-based electronic clinical attachments (Da Vinci CDex IG or equivalent) — and does the regulatory timeline confirm whether cliqueue's EDI adapter is a temporary bridge or a permanent design element, and whether a `PayerAdapterInterface` forward-compatibility seam is warranted?

- **CMS-0053-F finalized March 24, 2026 (Federal Register 2026-05676) — first-ever HIPAA claims attachments final rule.** Effective May 26, 2026; compliance deadline May 26, 2028. Adopts X12 277 (attachment request) and X12 275 (attachment submission) + HL7 CDA for clinical documentation content. Covers "supporting clinical documentation such as medical records, imaging, clinical notes, telemedicine visit documentation and laboratory results" accompanying claims. ([Federal Register 2026-05676](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/03/24/2026-05676/administrative-simplification-adoption-of-standards-for-health-care-claims-attachments-transactions); [CMS fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/administrative-simplification-adoption-standards-health-care-claims-attachments-transactions); [CMS press release](https://www.cms.gov/files/document/nsg-attachments-final-rule-press-release-updated.pdf))

- **CMS-0053-F adopts X12/CDA, not FHIR CDex.** The rule explicitly does not adopt Da Vinci CDex as the HIPAA claims attachments standard. It also does not cover prior authorization attachments — those remain outside the scope of this rulemaking. FHIR-based attachments exchange is neither mandated nor referenced in the final rule. ([CMS fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/administrative-simplification-adoption-standards-health-care-claims-attachments-transactions); [Reed Smith analysis](https://www.reedsmith.com/our-insights/blogs/viewpoints/102mo5z/cms-new-final-rule-on-electronic-claims-attachments-what-it-means-for-radiology))

- **Da Vinci CDex is STU 2.1.0 (published 2025-02-11) — not incorporated in any CMS rule.** The HL7 CDex IG at `https://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-cdex/` is Version 2.1.0, ballot status "Trial-use." It is not referenced in CMS-0053-F, CMS-0057-F, or any other finalized CMS rule. It remains a vendor-optional IG with active pilots (HL7 Da Vinci Trebuchet) but no mandatory adoption obligation. ([HL7 CDex IG](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-cdex/); confirmed via WebFetch 2026-05-17)

- **EDI 837/835 adapter is a durable long-term requirement — not a temporary bridge — through at least May 2028.** CMS-0053-F anchors X12/CDA for claims attachments with a 2028 compliance deadline. CMS-0057-F (Jan 2027) covers prior-auth and data-access FHIR APIs but not claim submission or adjudication. No CMS rule as of May 2026 requires payers to accept FHIR-based claim submission. The X12 837 remains the sole mandated submission format. The 2028 deadline extends the EDI adapter's required lifespan by at least 2 years beyond MVP. ([MGMA analysis](https://www.mgma.com/federal-policy-resources/april-20-2026-electronic-claims-attachment-transaction-standards); [CMS-0057-F fact sheet](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/cms-interoperability-prior-authorization-final-rule-cms-0057-f))

- **No FHIR CDex attachments NPRM identified in CMS's 2024–2026 Unified Regulatory Agenda.** The claims-attachments regulatory effort was completed via the X12/CDA route under CMS-0053-F. A separate FHIR CDex-based NPRM has not been published or announced in the semiannual regulatory agenda cycle. Claim uncertain: Unified Agenda entries are periodically updated; the absence of a CDex NPRM as of May 2026 is the best available evidence but cannot be guaranteed complete.

- **`PayerAdapterInterface` with pluggable X12/CDA + future FHIR CDex seam is the architecturally correct design.** The settlement layer should be agnostic to payer attachment format. The adapter boundary should encapsulate: X12 837P/I (claim submission), X12 835 (remittance), X12 275/277 + CDA (attachments per CMS-0053-F). Exposing a typed `PayerAdapterInterface` allows per-payer FHIR CDex support to be added without touching the on-chain settlement layer, if/when voluntary FHIR adoption matures at specific payer partners. This follows the same pluggable-adapter pattern already used in cliqueue's CDS Hooks client design.

**Design implication:** cliqueue's EDI adapter spec must treat X12 837/835 + X12 275/277 + HL7 CDA as the mandatory integration targets through at least May 2028. Define a `PayerAdapterInterface` abstract class in the off-chain agent spec now, with CDA/X12 as the production-default implementation and a documented FHIR CDex extension point for voluntary per-payer adoption post-2028. The adapter is not a temporary shim — it is a core spec artifact requiring formal versioning and a hospital onboarding integration guide.
