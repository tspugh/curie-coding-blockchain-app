# RFI Denial Pathway: On-Chain `paAuthHash` Scope and Limitations

## 2026-05-17 — Does cliqueue's on-chain `ClaimSubmitted` event + `paAuthHash` attachment commitment directly address the RFI denial pathway — can a payer treat this as pre-submitted attachment, eliminating the 90-day RFI delay?

**Short answer: No, but partially.** The on-chain `paAuthHash` is a provenance anchor, not a documentation substitute. It eliminates "we never received docs" disputes and accelerates 277/275 exchange post-May 2028, but does not eliminate the payer's right to issue a formal X12 277 RFI and deny if the provider doesn't respond with the actual document.

---

- **RFI denial mechanics**: An RFI denial (payer pends or denies a claim citing missing, incomplete, or insufficient supporting documentation) is triggered when the payer's adjudication system cannot satisfy coverage, medical necessity, or authorization requirements from the claim alone. The five documented causes are: (1) missing or incomplete documentation, (2) failure to provide required remark code, (3) lack of necessary information, (4) inadequate supporting documentation, (5) failure to meet specific requirements. ([Advantum Health / Kodiak 2024 KPI Benchmark](https://advantumhealth.com/how-advantum-health-combats-rising-request-for-information-claim-denials/))

- **CMS-0053-F mandates the solicited 277/275 workflow — not unsolicited proactive attachment**: The rule (finalized March 24, 2026; Federal Register 2026-05676; compliance deadline May 26, 2028) adopts X12N 275 ("Additional Information to Support a Health Care Claim or Encounter") and X12N 277 ("Health Care Claim Request for Additional Information"). The CMS fact sheet describes a **request/response model**: payer issues 277 → provider responds with 275 attachment. The rule does NOT create a mandatory "unsolicited attachment intake" workflow requiring payers to treat proactively-submitted 275s as automatically satisfying documentation requirements. ([CMS-0053-F Fact Sheet](https://www.cms.gov/files/document/nsg-attachments-final-rule-fact-sheet.pdf); [Federal Register 2026-05676](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/03/24/2026-05676/administrative-simplification-adoption-of-standards-for-health-care-claims-attachments-transactions))

- **Proactive attachment does not eliminate the payer's 277 RFI right**: A payer can still issue a formal 277 request for additional information and deny if the provider does not respond to the 277 — even if the provider previously sent an unsolicited 275 at claim submission time. The rule creates electronic attachment standards; it does not give providers a safe harbor that every proactive 275 must be accepted as satisfying every future documentation request. (Perplexity synthesis of CMS-0053-F, verified against Federal Register 2026-05676)

- **No blockchain/hash-as-proof precedent**: No published payer policy (Aetna, UHC, Cigna, Humana) accepts a cryptographic hash as proof of documentation availability in lieu of producing the actual document in response to a 277 RFI. No healthcare revenue cycle literature from 2023–2026 establishes that a hash/audit trail eliminates the payer's RFI workflow. (Perplexity synthesis; no contradicting primary source found)

- **RFI denial rates are rising**: Initial RFI denial rates rose from 3.51% (2022) to 3.68% (2023) to 3.82% (first five months of 2024). Administrative costs nearly reached $2B in the first five months of 2024 (vs. $1.5B in 2022). Inpatient RFI denial rates were 4.8% higher than all of 2023 and 8.1% higher than all of 2022. ([Kodiak Solutions 2024 KPI Benchmarking Report](https://advantumhealth.com/how-advantum-health-combats-rising-request-for-information-claim-denials/))

- **On-chain `paAuthHash` addresses RFI denials indirectly via three mechanisms** (not direct elimination): (A) **Provenance anchor**: on-chain `paAuthHash` proves documentation existed and was committed to at claim submission time — eliminates "we never received docs" disputes in appeals (provider can show payer the on-chain timestamp + hash); (B) **Accelerated 277/275 exchange**: payer receiving a `ClaimSubmitted` event with a non-zero `paAuthHash` knows the document exists, can request it electronically via 277, and receives a 275 attachment — avoiding the multi-week fax/mail delay that caused the 90-day RFI window; (C) **CMS-0053-F compliance path**: post-May 2028, `@cliqueue/cda-attachments` enables the mandatory electronic 275 submission workflow — the 90-day RFI delay is a function of paper/fax workflows, not the payer's right to request documents; electronic 275 response turnaround is typically 24–72 hours.

- **The $14.4B RFI denial pipeline is addressable but with different framing**: cliqueue's `paAuthHash` does not eliminate RFI denials; it eliminates the delay caused by paper/fax attachment exchange and "documentation not received" disputes. The ROI claim should be framed as: "cliqueue converts 90-day paper RFI cycles to 24–72 hour electronic 275 exchanges post-May 2028, via on-chain provenance anchoring + `@cliqueue/cda-attachments`."

**Design implication:** cliqueue's marketing and ROI model must be corrected: the on-chain `paAuthHash` is a provenance anchor that eliminates "we never received it" disputes and enables electronic attachment exchange, not a mechanism that eliminates payer RFI rights. The hospital BAA and ROI calculator should frame the attachment value as "electronic 277/275 turnaround acceleration (90 days → 24-72 hours)" rather than "eliminates RFI denials." The `paAuthHash` still belongs in `ClaimSubmitted` for audit trail purposes — the design decision is confirmed; only the marketing claim changes.
