## 2026-05-16 — Somnia inference fee governance: is there advance notice before the 0.07 SOMI/invocation fee changes, and does the September 2026 cliff unlock require disclosure?

**Question investigated:** Has the Somnia platform published a formal commitment to advance notice before changing the 0.07 SOMI/agent LLM Inference price — and should cliqueue's deployment documentation include a monitoring step and disclosure language in the hospital BAA Settlement Finality Exhibit regarding fee-change risk?

- The 0.07 SOMI per-invocation LLM Inference fee is **hardcoded in runner software**, not governed on-chain. Somnia docs state explicitly: "Runners today use fixed per-type prices in their software (no on-chain price discovery yet — that will change)." The fee is described as "a stop-gap." ([Somnia Docs: Agent Gas Fees](https://docs.somnia.network/agents/invoking-agents/gas-fees))
- **No advance notice commitment exists.** The documentation contains no SLA, no governance process, no change notice requirement, and no timeline for when on-chain price discovery replaces fixed runner pricing. Fee can change unilaterally by Somnia's runner operators without any published protocol.
- The Validator Council controls **base gas economics** (block-level base fee via validator voting, adjusts every 10 blocks based on execution time). Agent inference fees are a separate runner-level pricing layer — Validator Council authority over "gas economics" does not clearly encompass runner inference fees. ([Somnia Docs: Tokens Governance](https://docs.somnia.network/concepts/tokenomics/tokens-governance); [Somnia Docs: Gas Fees](https://docs.somnia.network/concepts/tokenomics/gas-fees))
- **Governance trajectory:** Somnia's future design replaces fixed runner prices with "on-chain median reflecting actual resource consumption (container-seconds, tokens in/out, egress)." When this transition occurs, inference costs become dynamic and market-driven — SOMI reserve sizing will need to track actual cost medians, not a fixed 0.07/invocation constant.
- **September 2026 cliff unlock confirmed**: TGE was September 2, 2025. Team (11% of 1B = 110M SOMI), Investors (15.15% = 151.5M SOMI), Advisors (3.58% = 35.8M SOMI), and Launch Partners (15% = 150M SOMI) all carry 12-month cliffs with 0% at TGE — first monthly vesting tranches begin ~September 2, 2026. This represents ~44.73% of total supply (447.3M SOMI) beginning to flow into vesting from the same month. ([Somnia Docs: Allocation and Unlocks](https://docs.somnia.network/concepts/tokenomics/allocation-and-unlocks); [CryptoRank: SOMI Vesting](https://cryptorank.io/price/somnia/vesting))
- At current SOMI price (~$0.168), the monthly inference cost for 10,000 claims/day (sub=5) is ~$672/day × 30 = ~$20,160/month. A 50% SOMI price decline (structurally plausible post-cliff) does not directly raise the dollar cost — it raises the **SOMI quantity** hospitals must hold. If hospitals pre-purchased SOMI before the cliff, they could hold a depreciating asset. If they acquire post-cliff, they pay lower SOMI prices but need more tokens per dollar of reserve.
- The Somnia tokenomics overview documents a **50% fee burn** mechanism — each inference call permanently removes SOMI from supply, partially counteracting unlock-driven dilution. However, at 10,000 claims/day × 0.07 SOMI, the network burn rate from cliqueue alone would be 700 SOMI/day — negligible against 447M SOMI unlocking over 36 months (~415,000 SOMI/month vesting from the September 2026 cliff tranche alone).

**Design implication:** The deployment runbook and hospital BAA must include an explicit "Inference Fee Risk Disclosure" section: (1) the 0.07 SOMI fee is unilaterally changeable by Somnia runner operators with no advance notice commitment; (2) when Somnia transitions to dynamic on-chain pricing, cliqueue's reserve sizing formula must be updated to track the on-chain median rather than the fixed constant; (3) the September 2026 cliff unlock introduces structural SOMI price risk that affects reserve dollar value but not reserve SOMI quantity calculations. Hospitals should be advised to acquire SOMI post-cliff (post–September 2026) or implement a cost-averaging strategy rather than pre-buying a full 30-day buffer before the cliff. Cliqueue should subscribe to the Somnia governance channel and runner software release notes to detect fee changes before they affect hospital SOMI reserve sizing.

**Open questions generated:**
1. Should cliqueue publish a "Somnia Platform Dependency Risk Memo" that discloses both the runner fee governance gap and the September 2026 cliff unlock risk — so hospital procurement teams have documented external disclosure support before approving deployment?
2. When Somnia transitions to dynamic on-chain pricing, should cliqueue's SOMI price circuit breaker be extended to also cap the per-claim inference cost in dollar terms (not just SOMI terms) — so the circuit breaker catches both SOMI price spikes and Somnia fee increases simultaneously?
3. Should the hospital BAA's SOMI Reserve Exhibit specify that the 0.07 SOMI fee assumption is subject to Somnia platform changes — and that if the fee changes by more than 20%, cliqueue will issue an updated Reserve Exhibit within 10 business days?
